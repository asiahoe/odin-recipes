Blackened Salmon Fillets Recipe

Description

Fresh, wild-caught salmon seasoned with a homemade Cajun blend of spices and basted in butter. Pairs well with roasted asparagus and creamy mashed potatoes. Quick, and easy!

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp ground paprika
  • 1 tbsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground white pepper
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • ¼ tsp dried thyme
  • ¼ tsp dried basil
  • ¼ tsp dried oregano
  • 4 salmon fillets
  • ½ cup unsalted butter

Steps

  1. Mix the spices together in a small bowl.
  2. Remove any pin-bones from the fillets.
  3. Rinse and pat the fillets dry.
  4. Score the salmon's skin to prevent curling.
  5. Melt ¼ of the butter over medium-high heat.
  6. Turn off the flame and coat all sides of the fillets in butter.
  7. Sprinkle all sides of the fillets with the spice blend.
  8. Add additional 2 tbsps of butter to the pan over high heat.
  9. As the butter starts to brown, add the salmon skin-side down.
  10. Cook until the skin is crispy, about 4-6 minutes.
  11. Add remaining butter to the skillet, and flip fillets.
  12. Cook until blackened and fish flakes easily with a fork, 4-6 minutes.
